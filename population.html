Great Chinese Famine

China is the most populous nation in the world. In 1982, China’s Population reached the billion mark.
But according to this graph, you will realize that population decreased in 1959, 1960, and 1961. What happened?

Let take a look at the Birth Rate and Death Rate over the year.
The Death Rate was higher than the Birth Rate in 1960 and 1961.

So the natural growth rate was negative in 1960 and 1961.

This was affected by the Great China Famine. According to historian research, approximatelly 22～30 million people died. Based on History of Chinese Communist website, Chinese population decreased approximately 10 million in 1960.

What lead to the Great China Famine?

The first reason stated by Chinese official is different types of natural diseasters including flood and drought from 1958 to 1961. What did weather data show at that time?

The data addressed that threre were definitely severe natural diseasters from 1959 to 1961. But at the year of 1972, a similar level of natural diseaster, especially on drought, didn't cause the significant drop in food production. So there were other factors caused the famine.

Another reason is Big Leap Forward. Thie movement affectly negativelly on farmers and agriculture.

Mao Zedong also brought up the thought of surpassing Great Britain and catching up with the United States in 1958. He declared it would take China 3 years to surpass Great Britain.

This Goal didn't accomplished in 1960. Thanks to the Economic Reform in 1980s, China surpassed UK in terms of Total GDP in 2006, which actually took 48 years since the original deadline.

Great Leap Forward dragged so many people into steel production, including students, farmers, workers, government officers, police, soilders and so on. They used a techniques called Backyard Furnaces.

The steel production went high from 1958 and 1960. But the quality of the steel remained questionable

While people were suffering from the starvation, the newspaper actually showed farmers were producing more food than ever. In People's Daily 1958, the headline addressed there were 36,000(Jin)/Mu Crops produced, which is about 180,000 kg.

Eventually, all of these factors, weather, natural diseasters, big leap forward movement led to the tragedy of Great China Famine.

The history mirrors both thriving and calamity. Please remember the history and cherish the present
